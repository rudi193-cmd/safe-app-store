# NASA Archive — Sponsorship & 501(c)(3) Path
**North America Scootering Archive**
*Preserving 70+ years of scooter rally culture*

---

## Why This Matters

388,815 photos. 1,147 rallies. 1997–2013 documented — and growing.

scoot.net kept this history alive for 16 years and is now at risk of disappearing. The NASA Archive exists to make sure that never happens. What we're building is a permanent, searchable, community-governed archive of North American scooter rally culture — every event, every club, every person who showed up, broke down, and got rescued.

This is cultural infrastructure. It deserves institutional support.

---

## Funding Model: CPB/PBS Style

We do not run banner ads. We do not sell data. The archive belongs to the community that built it.

Sponsorship here is **acknowledgment, not advertising** — modeled on public broadcasting:

> *"NASA Archive is supported by [Organization], committed to preserving community history."*

Sponsors are listed on the site. Their support makes the archive possible. They don't direct content, don't get user data, and don't get placement in the archive itself.

## 501(c)(3) Path

### Why File

A 501(c)(3) designation makes NASA Archive:
- **Eligible for institutional grants** (NEA, IMLS, state arts councils, preservation funds)
- **A destination for tax-deductible donations** — sponsors can deduct contributions
- **Fundable by foundations** that cannot give to individuals or for-profits
- **Credible** for partnerships with universities, libraries, and cultural institutions

### Filing Path

**Option A: Independent Organization**
1. Incorporate as nonprofit in state of record (Delaware or home state)
2. File IRS Form 1023 (full) or 1023-EZ (if projected revenue < $50K/year)
3. File takes 3–6 months; can operate during review period
4. Ongoing: Form 990 annually, state filings, board governance

**Option B: Fiscal Sponsorship (Faster)**
Use an existing 501(c)(3) as fiscal sponsor while building the org:
- Organizations like **Fractured Atlas**, **Open Collective Foundation**, or **Southern Exposure** can house NASA Archive
- Donations go through fiscal sponsor's tax-exempt status
- You pay a fee (~5–8% of funds raised)
- Can switch to independent 501(c)(3) later without losing donor history
- **Timeline: 2–4 weeks to operational**

**Recommended:** Start with fiscal sponsorship (gets you grant-eligible in weeks), file for independent 501(c)(3) in parallel when you have a clear board and budget.

## Grant Application Narrative (Draft)

> The North America Scootering Archive (NASA) is a community-governed digital preservation project documenting 70+ years of scooter rally culture across the continent. Beginning with the rescue of 388,815 photographs from scoot.net (1997–2013) before they disappear, NASA Archive is building the first permanent, searchable record of this community: its rallies, clubs, shops, people, and oral histories.
>
> Scooter rally culture represents a distinct strand of American vernacular culture — working-class, geographically distributed, deeply community-governed, and historically underdocumented. These are not hobbyists. These are communities that rescued each other on roadsides, named each other at campfires, buried each other with full ceremony, and kept showing up for 40 years.
>
> NASA Archive preserves what is already there — public records, photographs, oral histories — and makes it permanently accessible and searchable. We use AI-assisted oral history capture to interview community members and connect their stories to the documented record. Every fact is sourced. Every correction is welcome. The community owns the archive.
