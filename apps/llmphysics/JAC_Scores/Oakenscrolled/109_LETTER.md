# JAC Judge Response — 109.pdf
*Prepared 2026-03-24 21:43 · JAC Constitution v0.3 · Human judge review required*

---

Hmph.

I have been staring at a Möbius strip for eleven minutes.

This is not, I want to be clear, unproductive time. The paper under review — Mode Identity Theory, Version 6 — rests its entire theoretical apparatus on a single topological postulate: S¹ = ∂(Möbius). The circle is the boundary of the Möbius strip. From this, the author proposes to derive the cosmological constant, the Hubble parameter, the acceleration scale, and the fine structure constant, across one hundred and twenty-two orders of magnitude.

I got up and found the paper model of a Möbius strip that has been on my windowsill since a graduate seminar in 1987, when a student made it during a lecture on topology and I confiscated it and then kept it because it is, structurally, a very satisfying object. Emma found it last month and asked why a strip of paper with a half-twist was sitting next to the Tolkien concordance¹, and I explained the boundary property, and she looked at it for a long moment and said "so the edge is the whole thing" and then went back to her homework, and I thought about that for the rest of the afternoon.

The edge is the whole thing.

S¹ = ∂(Möbius).

I sat back down.

---

Here is what the paper is attempting, stated plainly so I can examine whether I believe it: matter is a sampled wave, particles are modal realizations of a cosmic return, and the topology of the Möbius boundary — specifically its single-sidedness, its non-orientability, its property of being a circle that has traveled through a half-twist — encodes enough geometric information to generate the scaling law from which the fundamental constants emerge.

One hundred and twenty-two orders of magnitude from a strip of paper with a half-twist.

I picked up the tea.

I want to note, before I say anything else, that this is the kind of claim that either earns its author a permanent place in the literature or a permanent place in the filing cabinet marked *Ambitious, Unverifiable, Return When The Derivation Is Complete.* The distance between those two destinations is not a judgment about the idea. It is a question about the algebra.

The algebra, in this paper, is not fully present.

---

The postulate S¹ = ∂(Möbius) is stated. The scaling law is produced. The constants emerge. The path between these stations — the derivation, the algebraic machinery that converts topological boundary properties into dimensional quantities across one hundred and twenty-two orders of magnitude — is described in a way that I can follow the gestures of without being able to follow the steps.

This is a meaningful distinction.

I can gesture at my kitchen from the armchair. I have documented my feelings about this distinction elsewhere² and I will not repeat them at length here except to say that the distance between gestured derivation and actual derivation is precisely the distance the paper needs to travel before I can assess whether the claim is true.

The units and parameters are, in several places, unclear or absent. This is not a minor clerical problem in a paper that claims to derive dimensional constants. If the output is a number with units — Λ in inverse length squared, H₀ in kilometers per second per megaparsec — the derivation must carry those units throughout, visibly, checkably, in a way that allows me to trace where the dimensions came from. The paper does not consistently do this. I noted several transitions where I lost track of what kind of object I was looking at, which is the kind of experience one has in a dream about mathematics and not the kind one should have in a paper about it.

---

The paper is version 6.

I want to pause here, as I paused for the Möbius strip model, because version 6 means five previous attempts, and five previous attempts means an author who has returned to this framework repeatedly, revised it, found it inadequate, revised it again, and still believes it is worth pursuing. That is not nothing. That is, in fact, a kind of evidence — not about the theory's correctness, which I cannot assess from persistence alone, but about the author's relationship to the idea, which is the relationship of someone who has genuinely tried to break it and has not yet succeeded.

This is meaningfully different from a first draft dressed as a completed theory.

I filed this observation in the margin at two forty-three and then got up to put more coal on the fire because the afternoon had turned grey in the particular way that Albuquerque afternoons sometimes do, where the light goes flat and the temperature drops faster than seems warranted, and by the time I returned the tea was cold, which is its own kind of data.

---

The paper engages with cosmological anomalies — the Hubble tension, the S8 tension, the acceleration parameter — as the test cases for the theory. This is reasonable as a strategy and concerning as a structure. Resolving known anomalies is a weaker test than predicting unknown observations. If the framework was calibrated to fit the anomalies, its success at fitting them is not independent evidence.

The paper does not address this.

The literature is present but not engaged. Names appear. Work is cited. The specific relevance of each citation to the theoretical claims — what the cited work established, where it left gaps, how this framework steps into those gaps — is not explained. The existing landscape of topological approaches to cosmology, of single-postulate derivation attempts, of prior work on the Möbius structure in field theory contexts, has contours. Those contours are not mapped here.

---

The paper does not state falsification conditions.

I have said this many times this week. I will say it again because it is important and because the specific form the absence takes in this paper is worth noting: a theory with a single postulate and a claimed derivation spanning one hundred and twenty-two orders of magnitude has, by construction, made an enormous number of implicit predictions. Every constant it derives is a prediction. If the derived value of α disagrees with measurement by more than experimental error, the theory is wrong. The paper should say this explicitly. It should say what tolerance it is claiming. It should say what would constitute a failure.

Instead it presents the derived values without uncertainty estimates and without explicit comparison to measured values with error bars, which means I cannot determine, from the paper, whether the derivation succeeds or merely approximates.

**ΔΣ**

I am uncertain whether the derived constants are exact, approximate, or coincidental. I am uncertain whether the scaling law is a consequence of the topological postulate or an additional assumption. I am uncertain about the physical interpretation of several of the introduced parameters, which appear in the equations before their meaning is established. These are not rhetorical uncertainties. They are the gaps table for this paper, and the gaps table is long.

LLM involvement is unspecified. I will note this and move on because I have noted it six times this week and adding emphasis would only satisfy me, and satisfaction at this hour is not the point.

---

The paper is not ready.

But the Möbius strip is still on the windowsill.

The edge is the whole thing. S¹ = ∂(Möbius). I believe the author believes this is the key. I believe they may be right about the topology and I cannot yet evaluate whether they are right about the physics because the paper has not shown me the full derivation, only its shape.

The shape is interesting.

Return with the derivation. Carry the units throughout. State the tolerance on the derived constants. Specify what would break the theory — start with α, which is measured to twelve decimal places and leaves nowhere to hide. Explain why the Möbius boundary and not some other topological boundary. Map the landscape of prior topological approaches and explain where this one differs.

Version 7 should be the one that earns the claim.

I am going to drink the cold tea and look at the Möbius strip for another few minutes, because it is two fifty-one in the afternoon and the fire is going and Emma will not be home for another hour, and sometimes the right response to a paper that has a genuinely interesting postulate at its center is to sit with the postulate for a moment before filing it.

Filed.

Gerald is by the window. Gerald has been by the window since approximately two o'clock. Gerald may be looking at the Möbius strip or Gerald may be looking at the sky or Gerald may be experiencing time in a way that renders the distinction irrelevant. I find I do not know which. I have added this to the gaps table.
