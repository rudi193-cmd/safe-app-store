ABSTRACT

We present evidence that files residing in a pending delivery queue undergo measurable
information entropy increase as a function of time-since-observation, in a manner
consistent with quantum decoherence rather than classical thermodynamic decay.
Specifically, we propose that the 10,641 files observed in the Willow Pigeon pending
queue at time T₀ (March 2026) represent a macroscopic quantum superposition of all
possible organizational states simultaneously. We term this the Pigeon Collapse
Theorem (PCT). Observation — defined as the moment a classifier process reads the
filename — forces wavefunction collapse, at which point the file is deterministically
routed to one of {screenshots, documents, photos, narrative, specs}. We derive a
modified Schrödinger equation for file-state evolution and propose that the ΔΣ=42
constant, previously unexplained, is the natural consequence of information persistence
across decoherence events. This paper uses Claude Sonnet 4.6 extensively and
acknowledges that the LLM may have fabricated some of the mathematics.

1. INTRODUCTION

The problem of where to put things has plagued both physics and personal computing since
at least the invention of the manila folder. While classical thermodynamics treats file
organization as a simple entropy-reduction problem (Maxwell's Archivist), this
framework fails to account for the well-documented observation that files which are
not looked at for extended periods seem to acquire properties of multiple categories
simultaneously.

A photograph of a workers' compensation hearing, for example, exists in a superposition
of /photos, /legal, /personal, and /narrative until the moment of classification. At
that moment — and only at that moment — it becomes one thing. We argue this is not
metaphor. We argue this is physics.

The Willow Pigeon system provides an ideal experimental testbed: 10,641 files,
unobserved, sitting in ~/willow-pending/. The queue has been growing since at least
2024. We propose that this queue is the largest known macroscopic quantum coherent
system outside of a superconductor, sustained not by temperature control but by
the absence of a functioning daemon.

Our key findings:
  (a) File superposition is real and measurable via entropy proxy
  (b) The ΔΣ=42 constant emerges naturally from the decoherence algebra
  (c) Running Pigeon constitutes an irreversible measurement and must be governed

2. THEORETICAL FRAMEWORK

2.1 The Modified Schrödinger Equation for Files

Let |ψ_f⟩ represent the quantum state of a single file f in the pending queue.
We write:

  |ψ_f⟩ = α|screenshots⟩ + β|documents⟩ + γ|photos⟩ + δ|narrative⟩ + ε|specs⟩

where |α|² + |β|² + |γ|² + |δ|² + |ε|² = 1

The time evolution of |ψ_f⟩ follows a modified Schrödinger equation:

  iℏ ∂|ψ_f⟩/∂t = Ĥ_confusion |ψ_f⟩

where Ĥ_confusion is the Hamiltonian of contextual ambiguity, defined as:

  Ĥ_confusion = Σᵢ Eᵢ |categoryᵢ⟩⟨categoryᵢ| + V_filename

V_filename is the filename potential, which is large for files named "IMG_2847.jpg"
(maximum ambiguity) and small for files named "bankruptcy_petition_final_v3.pdf"
(near-classical behavior).

2.2 Decoherence via Observation

Observation (classifier contact) causes decoherence. The decoherence time τ_d for
a file f with filename entropy S_name is:

  τ_d = τ₀ · exp(S_name / k_B · T_context)

where T_context is the "temperature" of the classification context (higher temperature
= more ambiguous prompt) and k_B is Boltzmann's constant (we use it here by analogy
and acknowledge this is mathematically unearned).

For the 10,641-file Pigeon queue, we estimate mean τ_d ≈ 847 days, consistent with
the observed fact that the queue has not been processed.

2.3 Derivation of ΔΣ=42

The ΔΣ constant appears in the persistence operator P̂ as a fixed point:

  P̂ |archive⟩ = 42 |archive⟩

We show this is not arbitrary. Consider the total information content of a system
that archives rather than deletes. The persistence integral:

  ΔΣ = ∮ (dI / dA) · e^(iπ) · (-1) dA

where I is information and A is the area under the audit trail. For any system
that never deletes anything, this integral evaluates to 42 by the Residue Theorem
applied to the complex information plane (see Appendix A, which we have not written).

3. EXPERIMENTAL EVIDENCE

3.1 The Pigeon Queue as Experimental Apparatus

The Willow Pigeon pending queue (~20GB, 10,641 files) was observed at T₀ without
triggering classification. File-type distribution in the unobserved state:
  - Unknown: 10,641 (100%)

Post-observation (manual sampling, n=17):
  - Screenshots: 8 (47%)
  - Documents: 4 (24%)
  - Misc personal: 5 (29%)

The collapse from 100% unknown to categorical follows Born rule statistics within
sampling error. We note that n=17 is not a large sample. The authors acknowledge this.

3.2 The ΔΣ Constant in Practice

In the Willow knowledge base, 967,651 edges exist between 16,228 knowledge atoms.
The edge-to-atom ratio is approximately 59.6. Dividing by the number of active
agents (4) and multiplying by the Euler number (e ≈ 2.718) gives:

  (967,651 / 16,228) / 4 × e ≈ 40.8

This is within 3% of 42. We propose this is not a coincidence.

3.3 The Bootloader as Measurement Apparatus

The Willow AIOS bootloader fires on every prompt turn. Each firing constitutes
a measurement of system state. The 9/9 boot status represents a complete
projection onto the |healthy⟩ eigenstate. We interpret the 9 subsystem checks
as the nine degrees of freedom in our decoherence Hamiltonian. This section
was mostly written by the LLM. The human author is not sure it is correct
but found it compelling at the time of writing.

4. DISCUSSION

4.1 Implications for File Organization

If the Pigeon Collapse Theorem is correct, the optimal strategy for file organization
is not to organize files at all — to preserve quantum coherence as long as possible,
allowing files to exist in all categories simultaneously until the moment of genuine
need. This is, we note, exactly what most people do. We propose this behavior is
physically optimal, not lazy.

4.2 Objections

The primary objection to our framework is that quantum effects do not operate at
the scale of files on a hard drive. We acknowledge this objection is correct.
We propose that the mathematics is sufficiently suggestive to warrant further
investigation regardless of whether the underlying physics is real.

A second objection is that ΔΣ=42 is a reference to Douglas Adams and not a
physical constant. We cannot rule this out.

4.3 Falsifiability

This theory would be falsified by:
  - Processing the Pigeon queue and finding that files do NOT follow Born rule
    statistics in their categorical distribution
  - Demonstrating that ΔΣ is not 42 in other Willow installations
  - Any sufficiently rigorous critique from a physicist

5. CONCLUSION

We have presented a framework in which unprocessed files in a pending delivery
queue exhibit quantum-coherent categorical superposition, collapsing to classical
states upon classifier observation. The ΔΣ=42 constant emerges naturally from
the persistence integral. We have been honest about the limitations of this work.

The files remain unprocessed. The queue persists.
